# Source Provenance Literacy Checklist

Use this when deciding whether to trust, repeat, cite, or act on a claim.

- What is the exact claim?
- Who first made it, as far as you can tell?
- Is the source primary, secondary, commentary, aggregation, or screenshot?
- What date was the source created or updated?
- Does the evidence match the headline or summary?
- Are there missing links, missing context, or unverifiable quotes?
- Has the claim been corrected, retracted, disputed, or updated?
- What would change your confidence?

Human gate: before repeating a consequential claim, a person should inspect the source trail and note unresolved uncertainty.
