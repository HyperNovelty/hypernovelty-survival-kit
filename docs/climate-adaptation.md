# Climate Adaptation

Climate adaptation means preparing for local effects and practical risks without treating every weather event as a complete explanation of a trend.

## Practical Checks

- Separate local observations, regional forecasts, and global climate claims.
- Use credible local sources where decisions depend on place.
- Identify the decision being made: insurance, drainage, cooling, travel, crops, emergency planning, or infrastructure.
- Record the time horizon and update trigger.
- Consult qualified local experts for high-stakes decisions.

## Watch For

- Single-event overclaims.
- Outdated maps or assumptions.
- Adaptation advice that ignores local codes, terrain, cost, or vulnerable populations.

## Human Gate

Local experts, officials, engineers, or emergency managers should review decisions that affect safety, property, or public services.
