# Media Provenance

Media provenance means asking where a claim, image, quote, chart, or document came from and how it may have changed before reaching you.

## Practical Checks

- Find the earliest available source.
- Distinguish original reporting, commentary, aggregation, and screenshots.
- Check whether a quote or image has context.
- Look for edits, retractions, synthetic media indicators, and missing dates.
- Keep a source trail for claims that will be repeated.

## Watch For

- Screenshots without links.
- Old material presented as new.
- Headlines that are stronger than the evidence.
- AI-generated summaries that hide uncertainty or source quality.

## Human Gate

Before repeating a consequential claim, a person should verify the source trail and note what remains uncertain.
