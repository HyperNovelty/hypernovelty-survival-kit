# Quantum Literacy

Quantum literacy means understanding enough to avoid being misled by claims about quantum computing, encryption, sensing, simulation, or investment narratives.

## Practical Checks

- Ask what quantum technology is being claimed: computing, communication, sensing, materials, or metaphor.
- Look for a specific use case, current limitation, and credible source.
- Separate research progress from deployable product.
- Ask whether the claim depends on future hardware, error correction, cost reduction, or specialized expertise.
- Treat "quantum" as a technical word, not a magic credibility signal.

## Watch For

- Claims that use quantum language without a measurable capability.
- Timelines with no assumptions.
- Investment or product claims that skip current constraints.

## Human Gate

For procurement, investment, cybersecurity migration, or curriculum decisions, require review by someone with relevant technical expertise.
